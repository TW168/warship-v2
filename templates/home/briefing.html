About This Chart

What to look for

  • High median — This product consistently costs more per pound to ship.
  • Tall box (wide IQR) — Rates are inconsistent across loads. May indicate multiple carriers, lanes, or a rate that hasn't been locked in.
  • Outlier dot above the whisker — One load was charged well above the normal range. Review that BL for surcharges or mis-keyed freight.
  • Outlier dot below the whisker — Unusually cheap load. Could be a back-haul rate, credit, or data entry error.

How to read each box

  • Box edges — Q1 (25th percentile) to Q3 (75th percentile). The middle 50% of all loads for that product.
  • Center line — Median ¢/lb. Half of loads cost less, half cost more.
  • Whiskers — Extend to the farthest actual load still within 1.5× the box height (IQR) from each edge.
  • Dots — Individual loads beyond the whiskers. Statistically unusual rates.

Products sorted left → right by median ¢/lb (cheapest to most expensive).

Formula

¢/lb = Unit_Freight (already in ¢/lb)
Unit_Freight = freight cost ($) ÷ pick weight (lbs)

Recommended Actions
🎯

Prioritize Top Product for Carrier Performance

Why: Your #1 product represents 35% of total shipped weight. This concentrated volume gives you leverage in rate negotiations with carriers. Single-point failures (carrier delays, capacity issues) directly impact revenue.

Action: Establish a dedicated carrier performance SLA for this SKU. Monitor on-time delivery, load factor, and cost-per-pound weekly. Consider a secondary carrier as backup.

📈

Monitor Monthly Trends & Forecast Demand

Why: Top products show month-to-month variability. Understanding seasonal patterns (peaks in Q1/Q4 vs. troughs in summer) prevents stockouts and over-capacity charges. Growth products signal market opportunity; declining ones may indicate customer churn or product lifecycle issues.

Action: Review the monthly trend chart each month. Identify products growing >5% MoM (invest in carrier capacity) vs. declining >5% (investigate root cause, adjust forecasting). Share 3-month rolling forecast with carriers.

🔄

Manage Product Portfolio Concentration Risk

Why: You ship 300+ unique products per month, but the top 5–10 drive 50%+ of volume. Over-reliance on a few SKUs creates vulnerability: customer loss, supply disruption, or production issues on a single product cascades into underutilized freight capacity.

Action: Calculate the Herfindahl index of product concentration annually. If top-5 > 60% of volume, develop a product diversification strategy. Identify high-margin, lower-volume SKUs that could grow with marketing/sales focus.

📅

Plan Staffing & Carrier Agreements for Seasonality

Why: Your shipping volume fluctuates 30–50% month-to-month. Without planning, peak months create overtime labor costs and potential service failures, while troughs leave equipment and staff underutilized.

Action: Use historical monthly data to build a 12-month staffing budget. Negotiate flex-capacity agreements with top carriers (e.g., 10% overage rates). Schedule planned maintenance on equipment during trough months. Train cross-functional temp staff 3 weeks before peak season.

📈
